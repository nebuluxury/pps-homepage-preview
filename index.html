Custom vs. Factory Strings

What's the real difference between custom and factory strings?

Honestly, a good factory string and a good custom string are made from similar materials. The difference is in the build: a custom string is made for your bow, pre-stretched and served the way you want, and built one at a time instead of mass-produced. The biggest practical wins are consistency (less creep and peep rotation once it's settled), the exact colors you want, and being able to talk to the person who built it. We won't tell you a factory string is junk — we'll tell you a custom string is dialed in.

My bow shoots fine. Why would I replace the string?

Two main reasons: wear and personalization. Strings are a wear item — the serving frays, the string fuzzes, and performance drifts over time. If yours is a few years old, worn, or has been weathered, fresh strings restore consistency. The second reason is simple: you want your colors and a string built specifically for you.

Will custom strings make my bow faster?

We'll be straight with you: a string alone won't magically add a bunch of FPS. What a quality, properly pre-stretched string does is keep your speed and tune consistent shot to shot, and speed nocks can be tuned in to squeeze out a little more. The real gains are in consistency and reliability, not headline speed numbers.

Are custom strings worth the money?

If you want exact colors, a string built for your specific bow, less hand-tuning of peep twist, and a real person standing behind it — yes. If you just need the cheapest possible replacement and don't care about any of that, a factory string will get you by. We'd rather you buy the right thing than oversell you.

Materials & Quality

What material are your strings made from?

Confirm exact fibers We build with premium bowstring fibers (such as BCY 452X, X99, and Mercury) chosen for low creep and stability. The right material depends on your setup and how you shoot — tell us your bow and we'll recommend the best fit.

What does "pre-stretched" mean and why does it matter?

After a string is built, it's put under heavy tension to remove the initial stretch before it ever goes on your bow. A properly pre-stretched string settles in within a handful of shots and then holds its length — that's what keeps your peep, draw length, and tune from drifting after a week of shooting.

What is peep rotation / peep twist, and how do you prevent it?

It's when your peep sight doesn't return to face you at full draw because the string is still settling and twisting. We prevent it by building with quality material and pre-stretching properly so the string is stable before it ships. Once it's broken in (a few shots), your peep should line up the same every time.

Ordering & Specs

How do I order custom strings?

Send your bow make, model, and year plus any color preferences through the contact form (or the builder above). We'll confirm the exact specs with you, then build your set to match.

What if I don't know my exact string specs?

No problem at all — most people don't. Just tell us your bow brand, model, and year. We can look up the correct lengths, or we'll walk you through measuring your current string and cables. Direct communication is part of the service.

What if my bow is old or discontinued?

Still doable. Older bows just need a little more info — usually a measurement of your existing string and cables. Send us what you've got and we'll figure it out together.

Do you build for single-cam and dual-cam bows?

Yes. We build complete sets, single strings, and single cables for single-cam, hybrid-cam, and dual-cam compound bows. Just tell us your setup.

Can I order just one string or one cable?

Yes — single strings and single cables are available individually if you only need to replace one piece.

Colors & Customization

How many colors can I choose?

You can run a single solid color, a two-color twist, or add a contrasting serving color — plus options like camo. Pick what matches your bow or go bold; it's your build.

Can you match a specific color or my bow's camo?

We'll get as close as the available fiber colors allow. Tell us what you're trying to match and we'll recommend the best combination. Confirm available color list

Will the colors fade?

Quality fibers hold their color well with normal use. Like anything, heavy sun and weather over years will dull any string — regular waxing helps protect both color and performance.

Install, Care & Longevity

Can I install the strings myself?

If you have a bow press and know your way around it, yes. If not, most local pro shops will press your bow and install strings for a small fee. We're happy to talk you through the process either way.

How often should I replace my bowstrings?

It depends on how much you shoot and how you store your bow, but a common guideline is roughly once a year for active shooters, or sooner if you see fraying, fuzzing, or serving separation. We can set up a reminder so you're not guessing.

How do I take care of my strings?

Wax them regularly (not the served sections), keep your bow out of extreme heat like a hot car, and inspect for fraying. A little maintenance dramatically extends string life.

How long do they take to break in?

Because they're pre-stretched, our strings settle in within a handful of shots. We still recommend shooting a few dozen arrows and re-checking your peep and tune before a hunt or competition.
